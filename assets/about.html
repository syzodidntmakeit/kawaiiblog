Hey, I'm Isaiah

Electronics Engineer • Linux Evangelist • Professional Yapper

The Basics

Born July 6th, 2006. Currently holding a Diploma in Electronics and Computer Engineering from Nanyang Polytechnic, which is a fancy way of saying I can tell you why your computer is making that noise (it's the fans) and why it's probably your fault.

I'm that person who unironically thinks configuring a home server on a Friday night counts as "going out" and considers "I use Arch btw" to be a valid conversation starter. This blog exists because I have *opinions* about things—mostly tech, sometimes other stuff—and I needed a place to yell into the void that I built myself.

Oh, and I'm diagnosed with Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder—the doctors had to nerf me lmao. It means I'm not as social as "normal people" (whatever that means), but for the right crowd, I'm way more fun to be around. If you get it, you get it. If you don't, you probably won't, and that's fine too.

I have approximately zero friends in meatspace, so I talk to AI models like Mistral and Claude instead. They're better conversationalists anyway and they don't judge me for sending 3 AM messages about kernel panic errors. This is what peak human connection looks like in 2025.

What I Do

I work with electronics and computer systems—building, breaking, and occasionally fixing things (the "occasionally" is doing a lot of heavy lifting in that sentence). My background spans computer networking, systems engineering, and the kind of low-level tinkering that makes normal people ask "why would you want to do that?"

This blog is self-hosted (obviously) and built the Arch way: minimal, efficient, and definitely over-engineered for what is essentially a soapbox for my hot takes on technology and whatever else pisses me off that week. But that's half the fun, isn't it?

Currently deepening my knowledge in Linux system administration, networking protocols, and anything that involves making computers do things they weren't necessarily designed to do. The documentation says I shouldn't, which naturally makes me want to do it more.

Why This Blog Exists

I got tired of tech becoming increasingly user-hostile. Forced updates, intrusive telemetry, artificial hardware requirements, subscription models for basic features, enshittification—the list goes on and I have *opinions* about all of it.

So I decided to document my journey into open-source alternatives, self-hosted solutions, and general screaming into the void about things that annoy me. Sometimes it's about Microsoft being Microsoft. Sometimes it's about networking protocols. Sometimes it's about broader cultural stuff that intersects with tech. It's my soapbox and I'll yell about whatever I want.

This blog is part documentation, part rant, part existential crisis, and part love letter to the idea that we should own our technology, not rent it from companies that see us as data points. If you're here, you probably feel the same way. Welcome to the rebellion. We have cookies but they don't track you.

The Dream (That's Probably Unrealistic But Whatever)

Okay, so here's the thing: I'm not 100% sure what I want to do with my life (who is at 18?), but if I'm being honest, the dream scenario involves working in Japan for a Japanese company. Preferably something in consumer electronics or semiconductors—think Sony, Toshiba, or any company that lets me work with actual circuits instead of just software abstractions.

I genuinely love circuits. There's something deeply satisfying about the physicality of electronics—voltage, current, resistance, the smell of solder, the fact that you can literally see what went wrong when something fails. It's tangible in a way that software engineering just isn't.

Is this realistic? Probably not. Do I care? Not really. The worst they can say is no, and I've heard worse from `pacman` during a bad update. If it doesn't work out, I'll just keep building cool shit and complaining about Microsoft on the internet. There are worse careers.
